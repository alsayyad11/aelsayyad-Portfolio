How I Work

A Process Built for Outcomes

Structured, repeatable, and grounded in evidence — not just intuition.

01
Discover
User interviews, competitive audits, analytics deep-dives. No spec gets written until the problem is validated.
02
Define
Opportunity sizing, success metrics defined before design begins, prioritization using RICE or weighted scoring.
03
Deliver
Clear PRDs, sprint planning, daily standups, and a scope log that prevents feature creep.
04
Measure
Post-launch tracking against the metrics defined in step two. The product never stops improving.

The Problem

GPs sent referrals by fax or phone. Specialists had no structured intake. Patients fell through the gap — missing appointments, repeating tests, and receiving delayed care. No one had visibility into where a referral was in the process.

My Approach
Discovery: Shadowed 8 clinic coordinators across 3 specialties to map the real referral workflow

The Problem

After discharge, patients were largely on their own. They missed medications, misunderstood instructions, and returned to the ER for conditions that should have been caught in a follow-up call. Nurses were spending hours on manual phone follow-ups that didn't scale.

My Approach
Research: 20 interviews with nurses, care coordinators, and recently discharged patients across two hospital systems
Key finding: Patients wanted simpler instructions, not more reminders. Complexity was the adherence killer.
Core product: Daily WhatsApp/SMS check-ins with symptom triage, medication reminders, and escalation alerts to care teams
Smart escalation: ML-powered risk scoring flagged patients needing human outreach before they deteriorated
Outcome tracking: Nurses saw a real-time cohort dashboard instead of managing individual call lists
